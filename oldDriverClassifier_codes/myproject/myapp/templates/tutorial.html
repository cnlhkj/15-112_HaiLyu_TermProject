Tutorial of Old Driver Classifier

Hi! Welcome to Old Driver Classifier. You might wonder what you can do with this website. Well, this website would be really helpful if you want to use a dataset to train a classification model, and you want to have a quick overview of the performance of some popular models trained based on this model. Afer you load your dataset in the home page, you will see three buttons. Those buttons represent the three main functions of this website.

  • Auto-classification
  • Exploration
  • DIY

      • Below is a vedio showing the functions of the website. It would be helpful if you take a look at it.

      Auto-classification

      The old driver classifier pre-chose four popular machine learning algorithms: Naive Bayes, Decision Tree, Support Vector Machine and Random Forest. If you click on the the "classification" button, you will get:

      • The best model among those four models and its 10-fold cross-validation accuracy; (1 is the 100% correct and 0 is 0)
      • The receiver operating characteristic (ROC) curve of the best model;
      • The the report of all the four models;

      Simply speaking, the higher the 10-fold cross-validation accuracy is, the more powerful the model would be, and the closer the ROC curve is curved to the left top corner, the better the model would be. Anyway, after clicking on the "classification" button, you can say something like "hey, random forest works perfectly on our dataset" to your boss, and you and your boss will think you can get the shit done, and you are both very happy.

      Exploration

      This function is simple. It will give a histogram or barchart of the variable you chose. Also, it will produce pair plots of all the numeric variables in in the whole dataset, and the color label will be the variable you choose.

      If you could not understand the graphs, you might need to take some statistic courses. There are some links which might be helpful:

      • Introduction to Statistics: Descriptive Statistics

      But if you still could not understand what I was saying, then go fuck yourself please.

      DIY

      Say, you told your boss, that Support Vector Machine would be the best model for your dataset. But your boss might know something about machine learnning techniques. And he or she might ask, "Well, what is the exponent of the polykernel?" And you might feel like, "WTF?" But, no worries. The old driver classifer provides a DIY interface for three algorithms: Decision Tree, Support Vector Machine and Random Forest. Why no Naive Bayes? Because there is no paremater to tune for Naive Bayes.

      Anyway, if you or your boss want to tune some parameters on your own, feel free to click the DIY button. It will generate report of the performance for you. And you can also change the method if you would like to.

      If you want to learn more about the parameters, you can check the links below:

    About Old Driver

    Old Driver classifier is a small website designed for processing small dataset. It has three main funcionalities:

    • labelAuto-classification

      Find the best model among Naive Bayes, Decision Tree, Random Forest and Support Vector Machine for your dataset.

    • equalizerExploration

      Help you explore your dataset by proper visualization.

    • settings_ethernetDIY

      Tune parameters for the model by yourself.

    Hope this website can be an old driver who could help you speed up the process of data analytics!
